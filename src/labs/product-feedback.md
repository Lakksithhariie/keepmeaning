## 01 — The Vision

> *We steal the soul back from the machine.*

The world doesn't need another AI writing tool. It needs the opposite — a tool that actively fights AI homogenisation. Keep Meaning exists for one reason: to return writing to the person who wrote it.

In an era where ChatGPT rewrites everyone into the same voice, Keep Meaning is the counterforce. We take whatever someone writes — rough, imperfect, human — and return it cleaner, sharper, and unmistakably still theirs.

### What We Are
Keep Meaning is a **meaning preservator**. Not a grammar checker. Not an AI rewriter. A tool that understands your tone, your rhythm, your intent — and reflects it back, better.

### What We Are Not
- We are not ChatGPT. We do not rewrite you.
- We are not Grammarly. We do not flatten you.
- We are not ProWritingAid. We do not overwhelm you.

| The Problem | Our Answer |
|-------------|------------|
| Every AI writing tool changes what you meant. You get clean grammar but lose your voice, your specificity, your cadence. You sound like everyone else. | Keep Meaning fixes the grammar, preserves the intent, protects the voice. You sound like you — just the version of you that meant to say it this well. |

## 03 — How It Works

### The Three Pillars

**1. Protect the Anchor**
Every piece of writing has a core truth — the one thing the writer meant to say. Keep Meaning identifies this anchor before making any change and treats it as the non-negotiable centre of the output.

**2. The Anti-AI Purge**
We actively blacklist the vocabulary of AI homogenisation — "delve", "tapestry", "unleash", "nuanced", "vibrant" — and replace them with specificity. The output should never trigger an AI detector or a human one.

**3. Rhythm & Flow**
We write like a human breathes — asymmetrical rhythms, deliberate fragments, cadence that mirrors the original. Grammar is fixed. Voice is protected.

### The Voice Fingerprint — Coming Next

At sign-up, instead of a generic form, users answer 4–5 questions about their writing: paste something that sounded exactly like you, describe how you think, share a phrase you'd never say. These fragments train a personal voice model that makes every subsequent output more accurate to them specifically.

> *This is the moat. Not the prompt engineering — the accumulated voice data no competitor has because no competitor asked for it this way.*

- Win LinkedIn creator community as beachhead segment

### Phase 2 — Monetisation Readiness
The transition from free to paid must feel earned, not trapped. The groundwork to lay now:

### Phase 3 — Expansion
- From LinkedIn creators to professionals, then students, then global non-native writers
- API access for teams and tools that want to embed voice-safe editing
- Voice graph as a data product — aggregate insight into how humans actually write vs. how AI rewrites them

### The One Metric That Matters Right Now

**Return rate within 7 days.**

Grammar tools have a session problem — people use them once and disappear. If users come back within a week unprompted, the voice preservation is working. That's the signal to optimise for above everything else in beta.

## 05 — Design Principles

These are the rules we don't break — in product, in design, in every output the tool produces.

| Principle | What It Means |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Meaning over metric** | We don't optimise for readability scores. We optimise for whether the writer recognises themselves in the output. |
| **Voice is sacred** | No output should trigger an AI detector — human or algorithmic. If it sounds like the machine wrote it, we failed. |
| **Invisible architecture** | The best tools disappear. Users should feel the result, not see the machinery. |
| **Simple over powerful** | ProWritingAid has 20+ report types. We have one purpose. Constraint is a feature. |
| **Free until it hurts** | Beta is for trust-building. Don't gate what hasn't been earned yet. |
| **One beachhead, fully won** | Don't market to everyone. Be the definitive tool for LinkedIn creators first. |
